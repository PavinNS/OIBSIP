Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Biography

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, popularly known as A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.

Dr. Kalam made significant contributions to the field of space research and missile development in India. He played a crucial role in the development of India's civilian space program and military missile systems. He was also known as the "Missile Man of India."

Throughout his life, Dr. Kalam inspired millions with his vision for India's development and his belief in the potential of young people. He passed away on July 27, 2015, but his legacy continues to inspire generations.

Awards and Honors

  • Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award (1997)
  • Padma Bhushan (1981) and Padma Vibhushan (1990)
  • Indira Gandhi Award for National Integration (1997)
  • Von Braun Award (2013) from the National Space Society
